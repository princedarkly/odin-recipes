KATSUDON

Katsudon カツ丼 is a timeless, classic Japanese meal that mainly consists of tender pork, onions, and eggs. This dish is a classic donburi (rice bowl) with the main ingredients sitting atop a bowl of rice and enriched by a delicious umami sauce.

This is Japanese comfort food at its most modest and finest. Ittadakismasu! We hope you enjoy!

Ingredients

  • 3 ounces / 2 pieces of Fried Breaded Porkchops sliced into long strips
  • 2 teaspoons white sugar
  • 2 teaspoons mirin (Japanese sweet wine)
  • 2/3 cupdashi stock made with dashi powder
  • 2 cups of shoyu (Japanese soy sauce)
  • 3 tablespoons mirin (Japanese rice wine)
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 small onion chopped
  • 2 large eggs beaten
  • 2 cups cooked white rice

Directions

    Part I

  1. Mix dashi, sugar, mirin, soy sauce, and salt together in a small bowl.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a small skillet over medium heat. Add onion; cook and stir until translucent, about 5 minutes.

    3. Part II

  3. Stir in dashi mixture. Lay pork slices carefully on top of the onions. Drizzle eggs around the slices.
  4. Cover skillet and cook until eggs are set, about 2 minutes.

    5. Part III

  5. Divide rice between 2 bowls. Top each with half the onions, eggs, and pork slices.

いただきます! Let's eat!
