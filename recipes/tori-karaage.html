TORI KARAAGE

Tori Karaage 鶏の唐揚げ is a timeless, classic Japanese meal that mainly consists of fried chicken served with lemon. This dish is a an all-time favorite that features moist, flavorful, boneless, skinless Japanese fried chicken with a super-thin coating that's incredibly crispy and crunchy.

This is Japanese comfort food at its most modest and finest. Ittadakismasu! We hope you enjoy!

Ingredients

  • 1 1/4 pounds boneless Chicken Thighs cut into two inch pieces
  • 1/8 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon white sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground salt
  • 1 clove garlic, finely grated
  • 1 tablespoon finely grated fresh ginger
  • 3 tablespoons cups of soy sauce (shoyu)
  • 1 tablespoon mirin (Japanese sweet wine)
  • 3 tablespoons sake
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper
  • 1 cup potato starch
  • 1 quart canola oil

Directions

    Part I

  1. Mix garlic, ginger, soy sauce, sake, mirin, sesame oil, black pepper, cayenne, sugar, and salt in a medium bowl.
  2. Place chicken in the marinade and mix until thoroughly and evenly coated. Cover and refrigerate for 1 to 10 hours.

    3. Part II

  3. Set up your dredging station: Place a wire rack over a foil-lined baking sheet. Add potato starch to a wide, shallow bowl.
  4. Remove chicken from the refrigerator. Stir to coat with marinade. Toss 2 or 3 pieces at a time in the potato starch until evenly coated.

    5. Part III

  5. Heat oil in a deep fryer to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C)
  6. Without crowding, lower chicken carefully into the hot oil in batches.
  7. Fry, tossing occasionally, until cooked through and the outside is crunchy and browned, 3 to 4 minutes. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate to drain.
